Core categories

MFA platforms (workforce)

Strong auth for employees/admins. The goal is phishing resistance for privileged actions and high-risk apps.

  • Prefer passkeys/FIDO2 or hardware-backed methods for admins.
  • Evaluate recovery + helpdesk reset controls (often the real weakness).
  • Rollout sequence: admins → high-risk apps → broader workforce.

Related: MFA rollout checklist →

SSO / workforce identity providers

Centralize login, policies, lifecycle, and visibility for internal apps. Reduce password sprawl.

  • Value is policy + lifecycle + visibility, not “single password.”
  • Confirm SAML/OIDC coverage and app ecosystem fit.
  • Plan break-glass access and outage modes.

Related: SSO for SMB →

IAM (cloud permissions)

Authorization for cloud resources. Misconfiguration creates catastrophic blast radius.

  • Least privilege + short-lived credentials where possible.
  • Audit privilege escalation paths and admin-equivalent roles.
  • Separate human, service, and automation identities.

CIAM (customer identity)

Login and identity for customers/end-users. Priorities differ: UX, conversion, fraud, privacy, recovery.

  • Decide: social login, passkeys, email magic links, MFA step-up.
  • Account recovery + fraud prevention are core requirements.
  • Threat model: takeover, bots, credential stuffing.

Related: IAM vs CIAM vs PAM →

PAM (privileged access)

High-risk access: admin sessions, production access, approvals, recording, just-in-time privilege.

  • Separate controls for production/admin operations.
  • Session recording/auditability for critical changes.
  • Approvals/step-up for privileged actions.

Device / posture & conditional access

Gate access based on device trust, posture, location, risk signals, and session context.

  • Prioritize privileged access + sensitive apps first.
  • Plan exceptions and break-glass flows.
  • Know what telemetry you actually have and can enforce.
