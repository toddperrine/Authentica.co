Vendors & Tools

Use this page to orient quickly: what each category is for, when it helps, where it fails, and what to ask vendors before you buy. Authentica is vendor-neutral; categories are broader than any single product.

MFA SSO IAM CIAM PAM Passkeys Conditional Access Device / Posture

MFA platforms (workforce)

Strong authentication for employees and admins. The goal is phishing resistance for privileged actions and high-risk apps.

  • Prefer FIDO2/passkeys or hardware-backed methods for admin access.
  • Evaluate recovery flows and helpdesk reset controls (often the real weakness).
  • Plan rollout: admins → high-risk apps → broader workforce.

Related: MFA rollout checklist, Modern attack patterns

SSO / Workforce identity providers

Centralizes login, policies, and lifecycle for internal apps. Helps reduce password sprawl and enforce consistent controls.

  • Value is policy + lifecycle + visibility, not “single password.”
  • Confirm standards support (SAML/OIDC) and app coverage.
  • Think through break-glass access and outage modes.

Related: SSO for SMB: when it’s worth it

IAM (cloud permissions)

Authorization and access control for cloud resources. Where misconfiguration creates catastrophic blast radius.

  • Use least privilege and short-lived credentials where possible.
  • Audit privilege escalation paths and admin-equivalent roles.
  • Separate human access, service access, and automation identities.

CIAM (customer identity)

Login and identity for customers/end-users. Priorities differ: UX, conversion, fraud, privacy, and account recovery.

  • Decide: social login, passkeys, email magic links, MFA step-up.
  • Account recovery and fraud prevention are core requirements.
  • Know your threat model: takeover, bots, credential stuffing.

Related: IAM vs CIAM vs PAM

PAM (privileged access)

Controls for high-risk access: production admins, sensitive data exports, infrastructure operations, and powerful SaaS roles.

  • Separate standing access from just-in-time privilege.
  • Require approvals/step-up for privileged actions.
  • Log and review: sessions, commands, and escalation events.

Related: IAM vs CIAM vs PAM

Passkeys (WebAuthn)

Phishing-resistant authentication using public key cryptography. Often the best “default” for reducing account takeover risk.

  • Best for: admin access, high-value accounts, and step-up flows.
  • Plan for onboarding and device changes (new phone / lost device).
  • Decide: passkey-only vs passkey-preferred with fallback.

Related: Passkeys: pragmatic guide

Conditional access

Dynamic policies based on risk, device, location, and behavior. Helps reduce session hijacking and limit blast radius.

  • Use for step-up on sensitive actions, not just “login gatekeeping.”
  • Prefer signals you can explain: device trust, MFA strength, geo anomalies.
  • Design exceptions and break-glass paths from day one.

Device trust / posture

Verifies a device is managed and healthy (OS version, encryption, EDR status) before granting access to sensitive apps.

  • Most valuable for admin consoles and customer-data systems.
  • Align identity + endpoint: MDM/EDR signals should feed access policy.
  • Plan for BYOD and contractors (segmentation + least privilege).

What to ask on every vendor call

Evaluate tools against real failure modes: recovery, helpdesk reset, break-glass, outages, and how you enforce stronger methods for privileged actions.
