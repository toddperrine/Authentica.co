Modern attack patterns: MFA fatigue, SIM swap, session theft

Many “MFA-protected” accounts still get taken over because attackers avoid the password step or bypass the second factor. Here are three common patterns and what controls materially help.

1) MFA fatigue (push bombing)

Attackers repeatedly trigger push prompts until a user approves out of annoyance or confusion.

  • Prefer number-matching or phishing-resistant methods (passkeys/FIDO2).
  • Rate-limit prompts; alert on repeated prompts.
  • Train users: “Unexpected prompt = deny + report.”

2) SIM swap (SMS interception)

Attackers socially engineer a mobile carrier to reassign a phone number, then receive SMS codes.

  • Avoid SMS as a high-assurance factor for sensitive accounts/actions.
  • Use authenticator apps, passkeys, or security keys for higher assurance.
  • Step-up policies for payout changes, password resets, or new device enrollment.

3) Session theft (token hijacking)

After successful login, attackers steal session tokens (via malware, browser compromise, or phishing proxies) and act as the user.

  • Shorter session lifetimes for privileged actions; re-auth for risky actions.
  • Device posture checks and conditional access where available.
  • Detect impossible travel/new device + step-up MFA.
