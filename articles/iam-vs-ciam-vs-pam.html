IAM vs CIAM vs PAM

These terms get blurred. Here are clean definitions and the architecture decisions they imply.

Back to Guides Browse vendor categories

IAM (workforce)

Employees/contractors: SSO, MFA, lifecycle, group/role mappings, conditional access.

CIAM (customer)

End users/customers: sign-up/login, social providers, consent, risk signals, account recovery.

PAM (privileged)

High-risk access: admin sessions, just-in-time privilege, vaulting, session recording, approvals.

Common patterns

  • Workforce: Entra/Okta/Google + conditional access + MFA enforced.
  • Customer: Auth0/Cognito/Firebase with step-up for sensitive actions.
  • Privileged: separate controls (PAM) for production access and admin operations.

Where teams get it wrong

  • Using workforce IAM as CIAM without considering user experience and scale.
  • Thinking MFA alone solves takeover (session theft and authorization bugs still win).
  • Granting standing admin privileges without step-up, approvals, or auditability.
