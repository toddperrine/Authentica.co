Vendor comparison rubric (use this on every call)

Most vendor evals go sideways because teams don’t define “success” up front. Use this rubric to force clarity: threat model, assurance, lifecycle, policy depth, UX, and cost drivers.

Back to Guides Browse vendor categories

Rubric

Use these prompts to drive a consistent, comparable evaluation.

Threat model

  • What attacks are you prioritizing?
  • Do you need phishing-resistant auth for certain roles/actions?
  • Do you need anomaly/risk signals?

Assurance and step-up

  • Can you require stronger methods for privileged tasks?
  • Can you re-auth on sensitive actions?
  • Can you enforce device trust/conditional access?

Lifecycle automation

  • SCIM provisioning/deprovisioning?
  • HRIS integration? Group rules?
  • Joiner/mover/leaver flows?

Policy depth

  • Conditional access rules (device, geo, risk)?
  • Session controls and token policies?
  • Admin controls and audit logs?

UX and recovery

  • Enrollment and recovery workflows?
  • Helpdesk reset controls and audit?
  • Customer sign-up/login friction (CIAM)?

Cost drivers

  • MAUs vs employees vs apps/integrations?
  • Feature tiers (conditional access, reporting)?
  • Support plans and contracts?

How to use this in practice

Start by aligning on the threat model and the “must not fail” workflows (recovery, resets, break-glass). Then evaluate vendors against those flows before you debate features.
